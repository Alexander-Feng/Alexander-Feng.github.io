About Me

Biography

Hello! I’m Alex Feng, a current student at Georgia Tech majoring in Electrical Engineering and specializing in circuit technology and electronic devices; I’m also minoring in Quantum Sciences and Technology. Before coming to Georgia Tech, my interests leaned towards more theoretical math and physics, leading me to completing the undergraduate math sequence (for engineers and physicists) in my Junior year of highschool. The following year, I took graduate level Abstract Algebra and competed in the USA Physics Olympiad, where I scored in the top 250 and won an Honorable Mention.

I wanted to increase my practical electrical engineering skills when I came to Georgia Tech, so I joined SiliconJackets as a member of the verification subteam. I wrote testbenches to verify that the RTL modules for our custom RISC-V CPU, which we successfully taped out, were working correctly. I later joined the digital design subteam to get a new perspective on the design process. In the future, I hope to explore other roles, including physical design. Additionally, I work in the Integrated Computational Electronics Laboratory, where I designed analog standard cells for field-programmable analog arrays (FPAAs), which are similar to FPGAs, but contain computational analog blocks. I’m also working on ASHES, which is a toolchain to synthesize a high level description of an analog system to an ASIC layout or FPAA switchlist.

On the theory side, I research theoretical atomic, molecular, and optical (AMO) physics with Dr. Sa de Melo. We analyze color-orbit-coupled SU(3) fermion dynamics, i.e., how time-dependent quantities such as orbital angular momentum change. I am also in the Quantum Engineering vertically integrated projects team, where we are developing software to find the optimal parameters for a trapped-ion quantum computer.
